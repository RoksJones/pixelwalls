1. Executive Summary

Pixelwalls is a decentralized, on-chain 1000×1000 digital canvas built on the Solana blockchain. The platform consists of exactly 1,000,000 unique, ownable pixels. Each pixel is minted as a non-fungible token (NFT) and lives permanently as a Program-Derived Account (PDA) on-chain.

Users claim pixels through a transparent, step-function bonding-curve pricing model that begins at 0.01 SOL and incrementally rises to 0.50 SOL. Once claimed, individual pixels can be freely traded, visually updated, transferred, or merged into larger composite assets known as Cluster NFTs (ranging from 2×2 up to 10×10 coordinate blocks).

Key Innovations:

  • Permanent Coordinate Reservation: Achieved via a specialized clustered = true flag, ensuring original pixel accounts remain alive to permanently prevent re-claiming.
  • Sustainable Treasury Split: A 90/10 primary sale revenue split between the project treasury and the agent wallet.
  • Embedded Royalties: A strict 5% royalty on all secondary market sales to fund ongoing platform development.
  • Social Verification: On-chain X (Twitter) handle verification and tiered social boosts.
  • Immutable Provenance: Full on-chain transaction and ownership history recorded via verifiable events.

Ultimately, Pixelwalls transforms a simple conceptual grid into a living, tradable, community-owned digital real-estate and art platform.

2. Introduction & Problem Statement

The markets for digital art and virtual land have experienced explosive growth over recent years. However, the foundational architecture of most existing digital canvas platforms suffers from significant shortcomings:

  • Centralized Control: Projects often rely on centralized servers to render, host, or validate state, undermining true digital ownership.
  • Temporary or Off-Chain Metadata: Many assets point to external, off-chain data sources that are susceptible to link rot or manipulation.
  • High Frictional Costs: Platforms built on legacy blockchains frequently price out users through prohibitive gas fees and poor user experiences (UX).
  • Lack of Scarcity and Permanence: Individual pixels or plots are rarely guaranteed persistent existence or absolute scarcity.

Pixelwalls systematically resolves these issues by anchoring every single pixel on-chain as its own distinct PDA, enforcing immutable ownership and coordinate scarcity forever. The 1,000,000 pixel limit is strictly hard-coded into the protocol—once the grid is fully claimed, no additional pixels can ever be created.

4. Why Solana

Solana was chosen as the foundational layer for Pixelwalls due to its high throughput, exceptionally low transaction fees, and rapid finality. An interactive digital canvas requires frequent state updates—such as claiming pixels, updating colors, or merging coordinates—which would be economically unviable on slower or more expensive networks. Solana provides the necessary infrastructure to deliver a seamless, high-frequency on-chain user experience without compromising the core tenets of decentralization or security.

5. Technical Architecture

The Pixelwalls protocol is a suite of smart contracts rigorously engineered to ensure state integrity and permanence.

  • Blockchain: Solana
  • Framework: Anchor (Rust)
  • Program ID: [To be set after first build; see deployment steps in contract]

Core Program-Derived Accounts (PDAs):

  • WallState: The global configuration account. It tracks total claimed pixels, manages the emergency pause switch, and identifies the program authority.
  • PixelSpace: Created once per coordinate (column, row). It stores the owner, color hex code, URL, NFT mint address, associated X (Twitter) data, boost tier, and the critical clustered flag.
  • ClusterSpace: Generated for every merged rectangular block (seeded by the top-left corner coordinate).

All PixelSpace and ClusterSpace accounts are designed as rent-bearing accounts that remain perpetually alive, even after being clustered. This intentional design choice acts as a mechanism to “burn” economic value and permanently reserve the global coordinates against future tampering or deletion.

7. NFT Integration

Every claimed pixel automatically serves as the foundation for a standard Metaplex NFT.

  • The associated NFT is minted via project infrastructure (backend) and orchestrates the recording of the mint address directly onto the blockchain using a `record_nft_mint` instruction.
  • The NFT mint address is stored immutably in the PDA as a write-once operation, cementing the bond between the coordinate state and the tradable token.
  • When users execute a merge operation, the protocol produces a single, unified Cluster NFT representing the aggregated coordinates.

8. Clustering System

The clustering system represents Pixelwalls' core innovation, enabling composable digital real estate.

Owners have the capability to merge any contiguous rectangular block of pixels they control—ranging from a minimum of 2×2 up to a maximum of 10×10 per transaction—into a unified ClusterSpace PDA and its corresponding Cluster NFT.

Design Philosophy & Mechanics:

  • Upon merging, the individual PixelSpace accounts are not destroyed. Instead, they remain alive on-chain but are permanently flagged with clustered = true.
  • This mechanic definitively reserves the coordinates, burning the rent-exemption SOL economically.
  • All standard mutations (e.g., color or metadata updates) on the individual underlying pixels are strictly blocked once clustered.
  • The newly minted Cluster NFT assumes the role of the primary tradable asset for that block.

This system generates provable scarcity, allowing for large-scale collaborative or artistic macro-works while immutably preserving the original pixel-level ownership history.

10. Social & Utility Features

Pixelwalls bridges raw asset ownership with verifiable digital identity.

  • X (Twitter) Verification: An authorized backend orchestrates OAuth verification, allowing users to bind their X handle and avatar to their coordinates. This verified data is secured on-chain.
  • Boost Tiers (1–4): Users can upgrade their pixels through tiers (Bronze → Silver → Gold → Wizard). Applied after off-chain $PIXEL payment confirmation, these boosts are currently cosmetic and social, serving to highlight prominent landholders and open avenues for future utility.
  • Customizable Metadata: Unless a pixel is locked via clustering, owners can independently update key fields directly on-chain, including Color (standard #rrggbb hex), external URL, and social identity markers.

11. Governance & Security

Smart contract integrity and risk mitigation are paramount.

  • Pause Switch: An emergency global pause function is available, tightly controlled by the designated authority to protect users during critical events.
  • Authority Transfer: The contract supports secure, one-way transfers of authority to new administrative wallets.
  • Hard-Coded Constraints: Treasury and Agent revenue routes are validated at compile time against hard-coded public keys, preventing malicious redirection of funds.
  • Mathematical Safety: All arithmetic logic utilizes checked operations with strict overflow and underflow protection.
  • Clustering Validation: The clustering engine performs rigorous checks, including full PDA derivation verification, strict owner validation, duplicate coordinate protection via BTreeSet, and enforced row-major ordering.
  • Anchor Best Practices: The protocol safely manages remaining_accounts mutations using explicit serialize calls on data borrows, ensuring maximum memory reliability.
